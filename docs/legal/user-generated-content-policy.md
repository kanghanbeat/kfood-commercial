# User-Generated Content Policy

Effective date: [Effective Date]

This policy explains what users may upload or share on K-Food Travel and how reports, blocks, and moderation reviews are handled.

## Allowed Content

Users may share food and travel photos, personal food diaries, restaurant experiences, regional K-Food discoveries, comments, recommendations, and profile content when they have the right to share it and it complies with this policy.

## Prohibited Content

Users must not upload or share:

- Illegal content or content that promotes illegal activity.
- Hateful content or attacks based on protected characteristics.
- Explicit sexual content or sexual exploitation.
- Violent, graphic, self-harm, dangerous, or harmful content.
- Harassment, bullying, threats, stalking, or targeted abuse.
- Defamation, impersonation, scams, spam, or deceptive content.
- Privacy-invading content, including private information without permission.
- Copyrighted, trademarked, or otherwise protected content without permission.
- Unauthorized photos or recordings of other people, especially where consent is required.
- Content that manipulates points, rankings, reviews, reports, or marketplace activity.

## User Responsibility For Photos

Users are responsible for ensuring that uploaded food, travel, and profile photos do not violate another person's privacy, publicity rights, copyright, trademark, restaurant rules, platform rules, or applicable law.

## Report Content Flow

Users should be able to report posts, images, comments, profiles, or other content that may violate this policy. A report should include a reason and may include optional details. Reports should be stored for moderator review with appropriate access controls.

Reported content will be reviewed as soon as reasonably possible, and clearly illegal, harmful, or policy-violating content may be restricted or removed promptly.

## Block User Flow

Users should be able to block other users. Blocking should persist using backend records when Supabase is available. Client-side filtering may be used as a temporary fallback, but it is not sufficient as the long-term moderation mechanism.

## Admin Review Flow

Moderators or admins should be able to review reports, inspect relevant content, update moderation status, record resolution notes, and take proportionate action. Moderation logs should be access-restricted and retained according to [Data Retention Period].

## Content Restriction And Removal

K-Food Travel may limit visibility, hide content during review, remove content, disable interactions, or restrict accounts when content violates this policy or creates legal, safety, privacy, or operational risk.

## Repeat Violations

Repeated or severe violations may result in warnings, temporary restrictions, loss of posting privileges, account suspension, or account termination.
